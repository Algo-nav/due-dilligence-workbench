## 1. Company Profile

**Founded:** 2007
**Headquarters:** Tel Aviv, Israel (US headquarters: Hoboken, New Jersey)
**Founders:** Yoni Assia, Ronen Assia, David Ring
**Ownership:** Public. NASDAQ: ETOR since 14 May 2025.
**Employees:** Not disclosed in source material.

Yoni Assia, his brother Ronen and David Ring founded the company in Tel Aviv in 2007 as RetailFX. It listed on Nasdaq on 14 May 2025 at $52 a share, raising about $620 million.

eToro holds licences with the FCA in the UK, CySEC in Cyprus, ASIC in Australia, US regulators, Abu Dhabi and several EU states. It serves roughly 40 million registered users and 4.23 million funded accounts across about 75 countries. Users trade equities, ETFs, commodities, currencies, CFDs and more than 150 cryptoassets across 25 stock exchanges. Copy trading sits at the centre of the product: users replicate the portfolios of other traders.

Q1 2026 was the company's strongest quarter since listing.

**Key facts**
- Q1 2026 net contribution: $258M, up 19% year on year
- Q1 2026 net income (GAAP): $82M, up 37%
- Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA: $109M, up 35%
- Funded accounts: 4.23M (May 2026), up 17%
- Assets under administration: $20.1B (May 2026), up 18%
- Cash and short-term investments: $1.3B
- FY2025: $868M net contribution, $216M net income
- Funded accounts by region: Europe and UK 72%, APAC 14%, Americas 10%, Middle East and Africa 4%

## 2. Business Model

### Business Model Canvas

**Value proposition:** A single app where retail investors trade equities, commodities, currencies, crypto and CFDs, and can copy the portfolios of successful traders instead of picking positions themselves. Breadth plus social proof, aimed at users who lack the time or confidence to trade unassisted.

**Key resources:** The copy-trading network and the community of traders that populates it. Regulatory licences across roughly 75 countries. The proprietary multi-asset platform. A $1.3 billion cash balance funding acquisitions and buybacks. The brand, and a registered base of about 40 million users.

**Key activities:** Platform engineering across five asset classes. Running the social and copy-trading network. AI product development, including the Tori agent built with xAI and Agent Portfolios. Regulatory licensing and compliance in every market. Acquisitions: Zengo, Bit2C and Spaceship.

**Channels:** Direct to consumer through mobile and web. Community and social features that generate organic acquisition, supported by paid marketing. The eToro App Store as a partner and ecosystem channel. Public APIs for third-party developers.

**Customer relationships:** Self-directed and community-mediated. Users interact with each other as much as with the platform, and copy trading turns other users into the product. Switching costs are low, so the community and the multi-product relationship through eToro Money carry the retention burden.

**Cost structure:** Cost of crypto revenue dominates the gross cost line and moves with crypto volume. Customer acquisition and marketing follow, and both are rising: adjusted operating expenses grew 7% quarter on quarter on higher acquisition costs. Then platform engineering and R&D, and compliance across every licensed jurisdiction.

### Revenue streams

eToro reports five streams as "net contribution", which reached $868M in FY2025 and $258M in Q1 2026 alone.

**Capital markets trading** (equities, commodities, currencies) now carries the business. It hit a record $166M in Q1 2026, up 71% year on year. Commodities alone drove about 60% of trading commissions.

**Crypto** produces enormous volume and thin margin. Crypto net trading contribution fell to $13M in Q1 2026, and crypto trades dropped 31% year on year by May. eToro keeps investing here through the Zengo acquisition and its on-chain roadmap, but crypto no longer drives profit.

**Net interest** on customer cash and margin balances contributed $48M in Q1 2026, down 5% year on year as rates eased. Interest-earning assets stood at $7.2B.

**eToro Money**, the neo-banking arm, reached $29M in Q1 2026, up from $22M a year earlier. Money transfers doubled year on year to $1.6B in May.

**Subscriptions and other** covers premium tiers at around $5 a month, the App Store and blockchain rewards.

## 3. Market Overview

eToro competes in retail online trading and investing, a large market whose revenue swings with retail participation, volatility and interest rates.

Retail money moved out of crypto and into commodities through the first half of 2026. eToro's asset breadth let it capture that rotation: capital markets trades rose 90% year on year in Q1 to 243 million, and total invested amount climbed to $50 billion from $39 billion.

A second shift matters more for margin. Automated and agentic investing is changing how users trade. Copy trading, Agent Portfolios and the App Store generate far more trades of far smaller size. Invested amount per trade fell about 25% to $197. Management confirmed that agentic and copy flows carry a lower take rate than self-directed trading. Engagement is climbing faster than revenue per trade.

**Key stats**
- Q1 2026 net contribution $258M, up 19%; adjusted EBITDA $109M, up 35%
- Capital markets net trading contribution: record $166M, up 71%
- Commodities: about 60% of Q1 2026 trading commissions
- Crypto net trading contribution: $13M
- Total trades: 253M in Q1 2026, up from 148M

## 4. Market Sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM)

*Analyst-constructed estimate. We built this from eToro's disclosed economics and stated penetration assumptions rather than third-party market data. Treat it as directional. A live engagement would triangulate against named paid sources.*

**TAM.** Global retail online trading and investing generates a revenue pool in the low tens of billions of dollars a year, spanning brokerage, CFDs, crypto intermediation and net interest income.

**SAM.** Multi-asset retail trading in the markets where eToro holds licences (UK, EU, Australia, US, GCC, APAC) accounts for an estimated mid-single-digit billions of annual revenue.

**SOM.** eToro captures roughly $1 billion on an annualised run rate, based on Q1 2026 net contribution of $258M. It converts about 11% of its 40 million registered users into 4.23 million funded accounts, which leaves the funnel as the largest untapped lever. Europe and the UK hold 72% of funded accounts, so APAC at 14%, the Americas at 10% and MEA at 4% all sit below their market weight.

Market size does not constrain this business. The question worth diligence is whether the revenue mix holds its take rate as trading shifts toward automated, lower-value transactions.

## 5. Industry Trends

**Volatility drives the sector, and nobody controls it (high impact).** The 2026 commodities surge produced eToro's record quarter. Asset breadth protects against rotation between classes. It offers nothing against a broad calm-market regime that suppresses activity everywhere at once.

**Agentic and automated retail investing (high impact).** Copy trading, AI agents and Agent Portfolios have already generated over 500,000 trades. They raise trade counts and cut invested amount per trade, and they carry a lower take rate. The shift cuts both ways.

**Rates are normalising (medium-high impact).** Net interest contribution fell 5% year on year in Q1 2026. A tailwind has become a mild headwind.

**Tokenisation and on-chain infrastructure (medium-high impact).** eToro bought Zengo, a self-custodial wallet provider, in April 2026 and has stated ambitions in perpetuals and prediction markets. The prize is large and the timing is unknown.

**Regulatory pressure on CFDs and crypto (medium-high impact).** UK and EU regulators keep targeting the highest-leverage retail products.

## 6. Competitor Landscape

**Robinhood.** *Strengths:* dominant US retail brand, large scale, strong options and crypto franchise, fast product cycles. *Weaknesses:* thin international footprint, no social or copy-trading network, heavy exposure to US regulatory and payment-for-order-flow dynamics.

**Plus500 (LSE: PLUS).** *Strengths:* the best operating efficiency in the sector at a 44% EBITDA margin, debt-free, disciplined capital returns, a growing US futures and clearing business. *Weaknesses:* its active customer base shrank 5% last year, its product set is narrower, and it has no community layer.

**IG Group.** *Strengths:* the largest UK-listed CFD and spread-betting incumbent, with scale, brand heritage, institutional reach and broad products. *Weaknesses:* a higher cost base, lower margins, slower innovation, weaker appeal to mobile-first retail users.

**Trading 212.** *Strengths:* zero-commission pricing that is winning users fast in Europe and the UK, where eToro holds 72% of its funded accounts. *Weaknesses:* narrower products, unproven profitability through a full cycle, no community features.

**XTB.** *Strengths:* strong position in Central and Eastern Europe, widening product range, competitive pricing, fast European growth. *Weaknesses:* geographic concentration and a smaller international brand.

**Coinbase.** *Strengths:* the deepest crypto liquidity, custody and institutional infrastructure, which threatens eToro's on-chain ambitions directly. *Weaknesses:* no equities or commodities offering, and earnings tied entirely to the crypto cycle that eToro has now diversified away from.

**Revolut.** *Strengths:* a very large retail base, a banking-first relationship, and the same super-app strategy eToro is pursuing, approached from the banking side. *Weaknesses:* shallower trading depth and weaker credibility with active traders.

eToro competes on network effects and asset breadth, not price. Commission-free challengers squeeze it from below, and super-apps and crypto natives press from either side. Q1 2026 showed the breadth is real: no single-asset competitor could have absorbed a crypto collapse and still posted a record quarter.

## 7. Customer Segments

**Copy and automated investors (the core, and growing).**
*Description:* Mainstream retail users who let other traders or AI agents make their decisions. The largest segment by count.
*Needs:* simplicity, social validation, low-effort investing.
*Buying behaviour:* small and frequent trades, with invested amount per trade down to about $197. High engagement. Increasingly served by copy trading and Agent Portfolios, which earn eToro a lower take rate than self-directed trading.

**Active multi-asset traders (highest value).**
*Description:* Self-directed users trading equities, commodities, currencies and CFDs across the platform's full breadth.
*Needs:* product breadth, execution quality, 24/7 access, advanced tools.
*Buying behaviour:* higher volume and higher value. This group drove the record $166M capital markets contribution and the commodities surge.

**Crypto-led users (contribution falling).**
*Description:* Users who came for crypto and stayed for it. Once the engagement engine, now a minor profit contributor at $13M in Q1 2026.
*Needs:* asset breadth across 150+ cryptoassets, self-custody through Zengo, on-chain access.
*Buying behaviour:* deeply cyclical. Crypto trades fell 31% year on year by May 2026. Valuable at the top of the funnel and for engagement, not for margin.

**eToro Money users (emerging, strategic).**
*Description:* Around 1.75 million users holding cards, IBANs and cash balances through the neo-banking arm.
*Needs:* cards, transfers, currency conversion, cash management.
*Buying behaviour:* transactional and habitual rather than speculative. Money transfers doubled year on year. This is the stickiest and least cyclical relationship eToro has.

## 8. SWOT Analysis

**Strengths**
- The diversification thesis survived a live test. Crypto contribution collapsed to $13M and the company still posted a record quarter.
- The copy-trading network gives eToro a moat competitors cannot buy.
- Record Q1 2026: $258M net contribution, $82M net income, $109M adjusted EBITDA.
- Licences across roughly 75 countries and $1.3B in cash funding acquisitions.
- Early AI positioning through Tori, Agent Portfolios and the App Store, with reported productivity gains.

**Weaknesses**
- Growth depends on volatility. A commodities surge produced the record quarter, and management said so.
- Take-rate quality is eroding. Invested amount per trade fell 25% to 48%, and copy and agentic flows earn less per trade.
- Customer acquisition costs are climbing. Operating expenses rose 7% quarter on quarter, and management attributed funded-account growth to marketing spend.
- Net interest contribution is now shrinking, down 5%.
- Europe and the UK hold 72% of funded accounts.

**Opportunities**
- Converting the funnel: 40 million registered users against 4.23 million funded accounts.
- Underweight regions: MEA at 4% of funded accounts, the Americas at 10%, APAC at 14%. The Abu Dhabi licence opens the Gulf.
- eToro Money, where transfers doubled year on year and revenue is uncorrelated with trading volatility.
- Tokenisation, perpetuals and prediction markets, accelerated by Zengo.
- The App Store and subscriptions as recurring revenue.

**Threats**
- A calm market. Low volatility suppresses activity in every asset class at once, and breadth is no defence.
- Trading 212 and XTB taking European users on price.
- Revolut converging from banking, Coinbase from crypto.
- Regulators tightening on CFDs and crypto.
- Operational concentration in Israel.

## 9. Porter's Five Forces

**Competitive rivalry: High.** CFD incumbents, commission-free challengers, crypto exchanges and neo-banks all compete for the same retail user.

**Threat of new entrants: Medium.** Licences and the copy-trading network deter newcomers, but large neo-banks and fintechs cross over with user bases already built.

**Buyer power: Medium-High.** Users switch platforms easily and compare prices. The community and multi-product relationships do the retention work.

**Supplier power: Medium.** eToro depends on liquidity providers, custodians and market-data vendors. Upstream venues set crypto intermediation economics, which explains why crypto margin stays thin.

**Threat of substitutes: Medium-High.** Robo-investing, direct crypto exchanges, prediction markets and traditional brokers each replace part of what eToro offers.

The market is attractive and crowded. eToro defends its position with network effects and breadth rather than cost, and Q1 2026 gave that defence its first real test.

## 10. Commercial Risks

**Volatility dependence.** *Severity: High. Likelihood: High.* Management attributed the record quarter to a commodities volatility surge. Multi-asset breadth protects against rotation between asset classes and does nothing against a calm-market regime that suppresses all of them together. This is now the primary risk in the business. *Mitigation:* grow non-cyclical revenue through eToro Money, subscriptions and interest to build a floor.

**Take-rate erosion.** *Severity: Medium-High. Likelihood: High.* Trade counts are exploding while invested amount per trade falls 25% to 48%. Management concedes agentic and copy take rates run below self-directed trading. *Mitigation:* subscription tiers, App Store monetisation, premium products.

**Rising customer acquisition cost.** *Severity: Medium-High. Likelihood: Medium-High.* Funded-account growth came from marketing spend, and operating expenses rose 7% quarter on quarter. *Mitigation:* convert the 40 million registered users organically and lean on the social flywheel.

**Falling interest income.** *Severity: Medium. Likelihood: High.* Net interest contribution is down 5% and will keep falling with rates. *Mitigation:* fee, subscription and Money revenue.

**Regulatory action on CFDs and crypto.** *Severity: High. Likelihood: Medium.* Core products remain regulatory targets in core markets. *Mitigation:* product and geographic diversification.

**Geographic concentration.** *Severity: Medium. Likelihood: Medium.* Europe and the UK hold 72% of funded accounts, exposing eToro to one regulatory and economic bloc. *Mitigation:* expansion into MEA, APAC and the Americas.

**Israel operations.** *Severity: Medium. Likelihood: Medium.* eToro lists its core operations and the regional conflict as disclosed risk factors. *Mitigation:* distributed operations.

## 11. Growth Opportunities

**Funnel conversion.** *Impact: High. Horizon: near-term.* Around 11% of registered users become funded accounts. Raising that number costs less than buying new users and cuts CAC dependence at the same time.

**eToro Money.** *Impact: High. Horizon: near to medium term.* Revenue reached $29M in Q1 2026, up from $22M, and transfers doubled year on year. It earns money whether or not markets move, which is the natural hedge against volatility dependence.

**Underweight geographies.** *Impact: High. Horizon: medium term.* MEA holds 4% of funded accounts and the Americas 10%, against 72% in Europe and the UK. The Abu Dhabi licence gives eToro a Gulf bridgehead.

**AI and the App Store.** *Impact: Medium-High. Horizon: medium term.* Tori, Agent Portfolios and the App Store drive engagement and could carry subscription revenue. They also dilute take rate, so how eToro prices them will decide whether they add or subtract.

**Tokenisation and perpetuals.** *Impact: High. Horizon: longer term.* Zengo and the on-chain roadmap position eToro for a shift whose timing nobody can call.

**Wealth management and subscriptions.** *Impact: Medium-High. Horizon: medium term.* Recurring, higher-margin revenue that cuts cyclicality.

## 12. Key Questions for Management

**On volatility:** How much of Q1 2026 came from the commodities volatility spike, and what does the P&L look like in a quiet quarter across every asset class?

**On take rate:** Invested amount per trade fell 25% to 48%, and agentic and copy flows earn less than self-directed trading. What is the net revenue effect once automated flows become the majority, and at what point does engagement growth stop covering the shortfall?

**On acquisition cost:** Management attributed funded-account growth to marketing spend. What are blended CAC, payback period, and the organic share of new funded accounts?

**On the funnel:** 40 million registered users, 4.23 million funded. Why has that ratio not improved, and what is the plan and target?

**On interest income:** Net interest contribution is down 5%. What happens under another 200 basis points of cuts?

**On geography:** Europe and the UK hold 72% of funded accounts. What is the investment case and timeline for MEA and the Americas?

**On crypto:** Crypto contribution is down to $13M. What is the return case for continued investment in Zengo, on-chain and perpetuals against redeploying that capital?

**On capital:** With $1.3B in cash, an active buyback and a live acquisition programme, what is the hurdle rate for deployment?

## 13. Executive Summary

**Assessment: Attractive. The diversification thesis has been proven. Growth still depends on volatility.**

eToro delivered its strongest quarter since listing. Q1 2026 net contribution reached $258M, up 19%. GAAP net income reached $82M, up 37%. Adjusted EBITDA reached $109M, up 35%. Funded accounts hit 4.23 million by May.

The obvious bear case has already been tested and it failed. Analysts have criticised eToro for years over crypto dependence, arguing that a thin-margin, cyclical revenue stream propped up the business. In Q1 2026 crypto net trading contribution fell to $13M and crypto trades dropped 31% year on year. eToro posted a record quarter anyway, because users rotated into commodities, which now drive around 60% of trading commissions, and capital markets contribution surged 71% to a record $166M. The multi-asset flywheel absorbed the shock.

The risk that remains sits one level deeper and reads worse. This business depends on volatility itself. Management attributed the quarter to a commodities volatility surge. Asset breadth protects eToro when money rotates between asset classes, and protects it not at all when markets go quiet and activity falls everywhere at once. Two pressures compound the problem. Take-rate quality is eroding as copy and agentic trading replace self-directed trading, cutting invested amount per trade by 25% to 48% and earning less on each one. And growth is bought rather than earned: management credited funded-account gains to marketing spend, with operating expenses up 7% quarter on quarter. The interest-income tailwind has meanwhile turned into a headwind, down 5%.

**Recommendation: proceed to deeper diligence on three questions.** First, model the through-cycle earnings profile in a low-volatility environment, which is the largest unknown in this business. Second, quantify the net monetisation effect of the shift toward automated trading, where engagement is rising faster than revenue per trade. Third, separate organic from paid funded-account growth and test whether the 40-million-user funnel converts without escalating CAC. The franchise is real, the diversification works, and the balance sheet is strong. What the valuation turns on is whether earnings hold up when markets go quiet.
